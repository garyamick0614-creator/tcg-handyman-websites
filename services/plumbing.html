Plumbing FAQ

The questions homeowners actually ask.

Do you do drain clogs?

Surface clogs (P-trap blockages, hair clogs, garbage-disposal jams) yes. Deep mainline clogs that need a sewer auger or hydro-jet, no — we’ll refer you to a drain specialist same-day.

What about a leaking pipe in the wall?

Slow leaks in accessible copper or PEX, yes. Mainline breaks, water-meter-side leaks, or anything that needs the city to shut off your water are out of scope. We’ll tell you the moment we see something we shouldn’t touch.

Can you replace my water heater?

Like-for-like residential swap (40 or 50 gallon, gas or electric), yes. Tankless conversion, gas-line resize, or new install — we partner with a licensed plumber for those.

Will my water be off the whole time?

Usually no — we shut off only the local supply valve under the sink or behind the toilet. The rest of the house keeps water. Water-heater jobs are the exception (hot side off for 2–4 hours).

What if you find something worse once you open it up?

We stop, show you, and re-quote in writing before any extra work. Common surprise: corroded shut-off valves that fail when you turn them. We’ll either replace the valve or coordinate with you and the city if the meter shut-off is needed.

Do you carry parts on the truck?

Yes — standard supply lines, gaskets, P-traps, flappers, fill valves, common faucet cartridges. We try not to leave for a parts run mid-job.
